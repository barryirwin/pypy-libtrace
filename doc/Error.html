Handling libtrace errors

Exceptions PltError and LibtraceError

These two python exceptions may be raised when pypy-libtrace detects an unrecoverable error.  Such errors are most likely to arise during operations on traces, see the Trace and OutputTrace page for further information.

For most purposes you can simply invoke the Trace and OutputTrace methods, knowing that if python-libtrace encounters an error it will throw a LibtraceError and exit, with pypy printing a diagnostic line to tell you what it thinks caused the problem.
Alternatively, you can catch the exception using a "try-except block," like this:

     try:
          f = plt.trace(ARGV[0])
     except:
          print "helpful message\n"
          sys.exc_clear()

Here, the sys.exc_clear() statement simply clears the exception history.
